Increase Conversions by 30%, 2020 study from Journal of Consumer Research

This study utilized data from actual consumer activity, not self-reported behaviors. You can read more about this study at Oxford Academic.

A 2020 study from the Journal of Consumer Research analyzed the impact of website design on consumer trust and engagement. The findings indicate that a professional, well-organized website significantly boosts user trust and engagement. When consumers encounter a clean and aesthetically pleasing website, they are 30% more likely to complete a purchase. This heightened trust and longer browsing times lead to higher overall satisfaction, increasing the probability of repeat visits.

In contrast, unprofessional websites with cluttered layouts and low-quality images see higher bounce rates, with users quickly leaving the site after viewing only one page. This demonstrates the critical role of professional website design in maintaining consumer interest and encouraging transactions.

For business owners, these findings underscore the importance of investing in high-quality web design. A professional online presence not only enhances brand credibility but also drives consumer behavior, leading to improved engagement, satisfaction, and conversion rates.

The study "The Role of Product Image Quality in E-commerce: Evidence from an Online Retailer" by S. M. Lee, published in the International Journal of Retail & Distribution Management (2018), focuses on how product image quality affects consumer behavior in e-commerce. The study controlled for other variables that could influence sales, such as product descriptions, pricing, and promotions.

Products within the same category were compared based on the quality of images used. High-quality images were characterized by high resolution, professional lighting, and multiple angles, while low-quality images were less clear and showed fewer perspectives.

The paramount importance of high quality imagery was affirmed. High-quality images led to a 40% increase in conversion rates compared to low-quality images,Surveys indicated that 50% of consumers were more likely to trust and make a purchase from retailers with high-quality images, and listings with high-resolution images had a 30% higher click-through rate.

CASE STUDY: Glossier's Community Building on Reddit

Founded in 2014 by Emily Weiss, Glossier started as an online-only company.

Glossier, a beauty and skincare brand, has invested significantly in building a strong community around its products, primarily using platforms like Reddit. Here’s an overview of their approach and the benefits they've reaped:

The company has embraced Reddit as a platform to engage directly with its customers. They participate in beauty and skincare subreddits, where they contribute to discussions, answer questions, and provide valuable insights into their products. This direct interaction helps Glossier maintain a close connection with their customer base and stay attuned to consumer preferences and trends​. Glossier encourages feedback from its community. By actively seeking and responding to customer input, the company fosters a sense of ownership and loyalty among its users.
